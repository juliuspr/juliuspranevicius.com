Do you want to take your horn playing skills to the next level? The Norwegian Academy of Music provides a stimulating environment to do just that.

Our Horn Studio offers excellent opportunities to reach your artistic goals. You'll get lots of performing opportunities, up-to-date pedagogic approach, international guests, teacher and student collaboration, in addition to the excellent facilities and a rich catalogue of elective courses.

You can learn more about [the Brass Department](https://nmh.no/en/departments/brass) and [Admissions](https://nmh.no/en/apply). We offer studies at the Bachelor and Master level. Studies in Norway are free of charge for EU/EEA [^fee]. For other students the fee is 220 000 kr per year (as of march 2026).

[^fee]: there is a small semester fee

> ⚠️ IMPORTANT: Application Deadline is **December 15th (!)** for the Academic Year starting in August/September of the following year.

## About the Horn Studio

The Studio has between 12 and 15 students, with 2-3 student in each cohort.

Tuition is organized with student-centered learning in mind. We focus on student engagement and active learning. In addition to the traditional individual lessons you will receive group lessons in various sizes - from small groups to full class.

In addition you the bachelor studies include a brass class, chamber music and orchestra. Academy's Brass and Woodwind ensembles regularly go on tours.

The two main teachers - [Julius Pranevicius](https://juliuspranevicius.com) and [Steinar Granmo Nilsen](https://nmh.no/en/contact-us/employees/steinar-granmo-nilsen) - work together to offer a full range of modern and historical performance skills. Please reach out to us if you have any questions. Stefan Dohr has been a guest professor since 2024.

## Focus of the tuition

Our tuition focuses on:

- developing a solid playing technique
- stimulating musical thinking and expression
- developing the professional skills
- adapting to each student's needs
- solving challenges in a positive, constructive way
- collaboration in the class

The Horn Studio has active collaborations with the staff of supporting disciplines, such as Alexander Technique.
